Research Highlights

AI-ML in Healthcare

Our AI-driven research utilizes advanced deep learning architectures, such as boundary-enhanced 3D U-Nets (BEAT-Net), to accurately segment complex tumor morphologies from multi-sequence MRI scans. This automates detection, providing highly precise, patient-specific 3D tumor models that serve as a robust foundation for customized treatment strategies. By seamlessly integrating machine learning algorithms into clinical workflows, we aim to greatly accelerate diagnostics, reduce human error in radiological assessments, and enable targeted thermal treatments for improved clinical decision-making.

Disease Modelling

We leverage high-fidelity Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) to accurately model complex heat transfer and fluid flow phenomena within biological environments. By simulating transient flow patterns, species transport through convection and diffusion, and Darcy's equations in porous tissue, we can reliably predict localized stresses and temperature evolution. This comprehensive modeling framework helps map out tumor necrosis during thermal therapies, validates computational predictions against experimental data, and provides deep insights into vascular hemodynamics for safer clinical hypothesis testing.

Synthetic Biology

Our synthetic biology research focuses on the optimization of superparamagnetic nanoparticles (MNPs) tailored for localized therapeutic applications. We rigorously analyze size-dependent magnetic properties and specific loss power (SLP) to maximize thermal energy delivery directly at tumor sites. By exploring different injection strategies and optimal MNP dosing for realistic breast tumor models, we aim to develop highly targeted magnetic hyperthermia protocols that efficiently destroy cancer cells while completely preserving the integrity of surrounding healthy tissues.

Medical Device Development

We successfully translate complex biophysical simulations and AI-driven predictive models into practical, life-saving medical devices and robust treatment protocols. Our team rigorously evaluates the performance of advanced thermo-fluid devices in clinical settings, ensuring highly accurate dosimetry and precise thermal control during magnetic hyperthermia therapies. By systematically testing heat dissipation and mapping thermal profiles in simulated environments, this research fundamentally refines device design and operational parameters, ultimately delivering reliable, non-invasive therapeutic tools for healthcare practitioners.
